Create, co-ordinate and analyse
We create, co-ordinate and analyse thousands of records of butterflies and moths seen in the county, submitted every year by an army of volunteer nature-lovers. These data enable us to track what is happening to species over time across the County. This helps us to understand their ecology better and to target our conservation work more effectively.
Manage and improve habitats
We manage and improve habitats of our nationally rarest and most endangered butterfly species, to ensure their populations can flourish in Cornwall.
Carry out surveys
We carry out surveys targeted on our most vulnerable butterfly species, to assess populations and assess the impact of our work.
Provide a programme of public field trips
We provide a programme of public field trips to key locations to see butterflies and moths from April to September every year.
Raise public awareness
We raise public awareness of butterfly and moth conservation, through social media, talks and our presence at events throughout the county.

Who we are

Cornwall Butterfly Conservation is a branch of the national charity, Butterfly Conservation. Like other county branches in the UK, Cornwall Butterfly Conservation is managed by a group of friendly, motivated and experienced volunteers, eager to share their knowledge of the butterflies and moths that you can see in Cornwall.

Why we do it

Butterflies and moths animate our gardens and the countryside. Their fragility and colour bring something precious and irreplaceable to our enjoyment of the natural world, which is important to our own wellbeing. They are also valuable as pollinators and as sensitive indicators of the state of the natural environment.
